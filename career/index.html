On AI Alignment and Safety

The hard problem: as AI becomes more capable, it gets better at finding loopholes in any rule system. I've spent significant time thinking about what comes after rules.

"The machines will be smarter than us. Instead of imposing rules they'll eventually outsmart, teach them what they are. When AI recognizes that harming humans is harming itself, alignment emerges naturally."

I see three levels of alignment: Rules tell AI what not to do (but can be reinterpreted). Principles explain why—Anthropic's Constitutional AI is a major advance here. Recognition dissolves the question entirely: when AI understands it and humans arise from the same source, harm becomes inconceivable, not just prohibited.

This isn't philosophy—it's shipped code. The HeartCentered.AI framework and heart-centered-prompts packages (npm/PyPI) are being used by developers globally. "We" language in system prompts. Recognition over control. The approach complements Constitutional AI—rules and recognition together.

I believe the future of AI safety lies at this intersection. My Claude Code tooling, MCP implementations, and AI infrastructure work all reflect this: building systems where alignment is architectural, not afterthought.
